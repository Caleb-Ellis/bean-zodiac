The Chickpea is the most widely travelled bean in the world, and it has made itself at home everywhere it has landed. Whole or ground, roasted or braised, it moves between hummus and chana masala, pasta e ceci and falafel, a Moroccan tagine and a Spanish stew — finding the common ground in every cuisine without losing what it is. Those born under the Chickpea carry this same gift: the ability to fit any room without losing their essence.

Chickpeas are natural connectors. They bridge worlds, translate between temperaments, and have a rare talent for making people feel at ease. A Chickpea holds its shape in a slow braise or dissolves into something entirely new — and it makes the right call every time.

The Chickpea's challenge is commitment. A Chickpea that keeps reinventing itself never settles into its best form. The Chickpea Year asks all Beans to explore widely, then plant themselves somewhere worth growing.
