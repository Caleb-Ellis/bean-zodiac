The Adzuki Bean has been used for centuries in East Asian cultures to mark good fortune, to celebrate new years and new lives, to sweeten the most auspicious occasions. Small and deep red, it carries within it the accumulated luck of countless celebrations. Those born under the Adzuki are drawn to joy the way a compass needle is drawn north: it is not a mood, it is an orientation.

You bring festivity with you wherever you go. Gatherings are better with you in them. You have a talent for recognizing what deserves to be celebrated and for compelling others to celebrate it with you. Your generosity — with time, attention, and good cheer — is your most distinctive quality.

The Adzuki's shadow side is the avoidance of difficulty. Celebration can become a way of outrunning what needs to be faced. The Adzuki year asks you to let joy be a response to life, not a substitute for it.

**Fortune for this year:** Your luck is real, but it needs to be met halfway. Take one calculated risk you have been treating as a wish.
