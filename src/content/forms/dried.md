Drying is reduction. The moisture that made the Bean soft and immediate is drawn out slowly until what remains is dense, light, inert — capable of sitting undisturbed on a shelf for years. Nothing is added. Only what is inessential is taken away.

Dried Beans carry a concentrated self that circumstance has not been able to dilute. They are not diminished by solitude or scarcity, but are built for exactly those conditions. Where others require warmth and company to function, the Dried Bean marches on.

The Dried Bean's shadow is brittleness. What cannot yield will eventually fracture. The Dried Bean asks you to remember that softness, under the right conditions, is always possible.
