Boiling is immersion. The Bean enters the water and is slowly, completely remade — softened to its core, its starch released, its hardness given over to the liquid surrounding it. There is a profound patience in the Boiled Bean.

Boiled Beans carry a deep composure. They are the sustaining ones — steady in crisis, reliable over long stretches, capable of the kind of consistent presence that rarer temperaments cannot maintain. Others come to them to be restored.

The Boiled Bean's shadow is despondency. The same capacity for stillness can tip into a heaviness that is difficult to lift. The Boiled Season asks you to be still — not to sink.
