Roasting is the most generous of methods. It asks for nothing from the ingredient except to sit in warmth and become more fully itself. The heat is dry and patient and pervasive — it coaxes out sugars, deepens colour, fills the whole space with something irresistible. A roasted bean announces itself from across the room. It cannot help it.

People of the Roasted Method carry their warmth outward as a matter of nature. They are the ones who make a room feel inhabited, whose presence is felt before they've said anything, who seem to run a few degrees warmer than everyone else. They have a talent for pleasure — their own and others'. They know how to make an occasion out of an ordinary evening.

The Roasted Method's shadow is self-indulgence. Warmth sustained without discipline becomes excess. The Roasted Method asks you to pace yourself.
