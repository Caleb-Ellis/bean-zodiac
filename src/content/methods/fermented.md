Fermentation requires no fire, no outside force. It is transformation from within, driven by living organisms working on their own schedule, according to their own logic, in conditions no one else can see. The result is something the original ingredient could never have predicted: sour, complex, alive in a way that fresh things are not. Fermentation is strange alchemy, and it only works if you leave it alone.

Beans of the Fermented Method do their most important work out of sight — processing, converting, arriving at conclusions that others cannot follow unless they've taken the same long road. They are not quick to share what they're working on. The transformation must complete before it can be presented. When it finally arrives, it is unlike anything anyone else has produced.

The Fermented Method's shadow is aberrance — the risk of going somewhere no one else can follow. Fermentation and spoilage use the same mechanism. The Fermented Method asks you not to get lost within yourself.
