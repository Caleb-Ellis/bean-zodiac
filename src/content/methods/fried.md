Frying is transformation by fire — fast, total, and irreversible. The oil is screaming hot before the Bean arrives. There is no slow build, no gentle acclimatisation. What goes in emerges immediately changed: sealed, vivid, crackling with energy. Frying does not coax. It decides.

Beans of the Fried Method are kinetic. They move through the world with a characteristic heat — decisive, passionate, difficult to ignore. They process experience through action rather than reflection: the doing is the thinking. They are often the ones who start something, who push past hesitation while others are still weighing options.

The Fried Method's shadow is irascibility. The same heat that transforms is the heat that burns. The Fried Method asks you to know the difference.
