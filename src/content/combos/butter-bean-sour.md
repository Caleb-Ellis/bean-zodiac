Sourness cuts through butter bean richness in exactly the right way — a splash of white wine vinegar in the slow braise, a wedge of lemon at the end — not to ruin the comfort but to make it honest. This year asks a gentle but specific question: are you offering warmth because people need it, or because a harder conversation feels too disruptive? Sour doesn't wreck the butter bean. It makes it taste like what it actually is. The honesty available this year is kinder than the comfort you'd substitute for it.

**Fortune for this year:** The truth is richer than the kindness you've been substituting for it.
