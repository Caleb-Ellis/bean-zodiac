People born in the Year of the Umami Navy Bean are the quietly indispensable souls around whom communities form and hold. They have the Navy Bean's foundational dependability and deep commitment to the people around them, deepened further by an Umami richness that makes their steady presence genuinely sustaining rather than merely reliable. They are not exciting to describe. They are completely essential in practice. The table they have been setting — for years, quietly, without announcement — is full of people who are there because of them, and the meal is extraordinary.
