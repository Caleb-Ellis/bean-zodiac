People born in the Year of the Sweet Black Bean are the great quiet sustainers — people who do foundational, invisible work that makes everything around them better, and who are sometimes genuinely surprised when the sweetness of life finds them. They carry depth as a baseline condition: the darkness under the brownie that makes it taste extraordinary rather than merely nice. Their complication is a tendency to be slightly suspicious of good fortune, to look for the catch in the easy year. There is no catch. The depth they carry is exactly what makes the sweetness satisfying rather than cloying.
