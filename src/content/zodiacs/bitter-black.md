People born in the Year of the Bitter Black Bean are the ones who take time to appreciate and longer still to forget. They do not show their hand early and they do not rush the reveal — there is a deep, patient complexity to them that resembles dark chocolate or a good mole: initially unexpected, quietly compelling, and impossible to ignore once you've properly tasted it. They have no interest in quick wins or fast friendships. What they build is slow and genuinely good, and the people who figure that out consider themselves fortunate.
