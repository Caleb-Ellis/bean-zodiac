Beans born in the Season of the Sour Roasted Butter Bean are the Bean Zodiac's most guileless — there is nothing behind the thing they say, no second meaning in the gesture, no version of their face that is not the true version.

Butter Bean has always been exactly what it appears; Sour has stripped away any affectation, any motive that does not simply want to be honest; Roasted brings this outward and fills the room with a warmth that cannot be faked, because these Beans have not learned how. They take people at their word because it does not occur to them to do otherwise, say what they mean, and wait to see what happens, without strategy. This should be naive — somehow it mostly isn't.

The Sour Roasted Butter Bean's challenge is recognising that the world is not always reciprocal. Guilelessness is a virtue, but it is not armour. These Beans must learn to notice when they are being read strategically without becoming strategic themselves.
