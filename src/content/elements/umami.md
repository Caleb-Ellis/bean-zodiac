Umami is the flavour that is hardest to name but impossible to mistake. It is depth, substance, the quality that makes you reach for one more bite before you have consciously processed what you are eating. Found in slow-cooked broths, aged cheeses, fermented pastes, dried mushrooms — umami is the flavour of patience made edible, of time transformed into richness.

A umami year carries this same quality of invisible depth. This is a year not for novelty but for substance. Rather than adding new things, the Umami year asks you to tend what is already there — to let relationships mature, projects develop, skills deepen. The richest experiences this year will come not from starting something new but from staying with something long enough to find its full flavour.

Umami does not announce itself the way spicy does. It works beneath the surface, enhancing everything around it, making the whole more coherent. You will not always know where the richness is coming from. That is the point.

Umami's shadow is complacency. Depth without movement becomes stagnation. The Umami year asks you to be still with purpose, not still out of inertia.

**Fortune for this year:** The thing you have been building quietly is more nourishing than you know. Let people taste it.
