The bitter flavour is the one that takes the longest to love. Children reject it; seasoned palates seek it out. A bitter year carries this same quality: it will not yield its gifts immediately, and those who expect easy reward will turn away. But those who lean into the complexity will find a richness that sweeter years cannot match.

Bitter years reward the long view. The coffee drinker who persisted through the first sour cup. The reader who stayed with the difficult book. The relationship that required real work before it opened. This is the flavour of acquired tastes — of things that require you to grow into them before they grow on you.

Bitterness in cooking is not failure; it is contrast. The dark chocolate that makes the caramel taste sweeter. The radicchio that makes the whole salad come alive. Your particular complexity this year will make everything around you more vivid, if you trust it.

Bitter's shadow is cynicism. There is a fine line between discernment and joylessness. The Bitter year asks you to develop your palate, not to sour on everything.

**Fortune for this year:** Don't rush to resolution. The thing you are learning to appreciate is becoming part of who you are.
